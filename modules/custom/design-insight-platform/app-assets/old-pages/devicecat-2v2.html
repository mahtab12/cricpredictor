United States

Market Highlights

Last updated 26 May 2015

Market Definitions

The US market for cardiac assist devices includes VADs, IABP systems, TAHs, and remote hemodynamic monitoring systems. These devices are explained in detail in Cardiac Assist Device Descriptions.

Market Highlights

  • The increasing prevalence of advanced heart failure and cardiogenic shock in the US as well as the increasing number of high-risk PCI procedures will result in increasing use of most cardiac assist devices in the US, with the exception of IAB catheters, where volumes will decline slightly.
  • The strong adoption of pVADs, such as the Impella device from Abiomed, will result in temporary VAD revenues surpassing durable VAD revenues in 2016 to become the largest segment in the US cardiac assist device market. Rapid growth in the temporary VAD segment will be supported by increasing product introductions, and new indications for their use.
  • Blood pressure monitoring in heart failure patients is important because of device-related and physiological complications associated with elevated blood pressure (Bennett M, 2015). As a result, companies are developing durable VADs that incorporate blood monitoring capabilities—like the HeartAssist5 from ReliantHeart—and are investigating further the effect of blood pressure monitoring on device-associated stroke rates in clinical trials, such as in the ENDURANCE II trial from HeartWare.
  • Targeting the larger yet less-sick NYHA Class III patient population remains difficult for most competitors in the US cardiac assist device market. Although CardioMEMS showed impressive uptake soon after introduction in 2014, unfavorable reimbursement decisions in 2015 and early 2016 by Medicare Administrative Contractors—due to their lack of confidence in the clinical benefit of CardioMEMS—have restrained further adoption in the US. Additionally, physicians and NYHA Class III patients remain skeptical about the benefits of durable VADs when weighed against the increased risk of adverse events like pump thrombosis and bleeding. As a result, several trials have been halted because of a lack of patient enrollment and safety concerns. However, percutaneous devices are in development that may address the concerns associated with implanting current generation VADs in less-sick patients.
  • Several new durable and temporary VAD devices will enter the US market in the first half of the forecast period. The HeartMate 3, MVAD, and CardiacAssist5 are all eagerly awaited by physicians due to their smaller sizes and improved pump designs, which may lead to decreased rates of device-related adverse events. Longevity and reliability of durable VADs will be paramount if they are to be increasingly used for DT. In addition to the HeartMate 3, St. Jude Medical will launch the first expandable pVAD in 2017, the HeartMate PHP, which will facilitate vascular access with its low-profile while supporting increased blood flow rates in its expanded state.
  • The increasing adoption of premium devices in both the IABP and temporary VAD segments, such as larger diameter IAB catheters and the Impella CP, will result in increasing aggregate ASPs in these segments of the US cardiac assist market, while other devices like durable VADs will experience pricing pressure due to their elevated ASPs.
