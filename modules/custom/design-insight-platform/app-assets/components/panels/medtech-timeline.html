  1. October 2015

    Thoratec/St. Jude Medical

    St. Jude Medical completes acquisition of Thoratec

  2. October 2015

    SynCardia Systems, Inc.

    Withdraws IPO

    SynCardia subsequently hires investment bank to advise its board of directors on strategic options

  3. December 2015

    St. Jude Medical - HeartMate 3

    Publishes HM3 CE Mark study results for HeartMate 3

    Results are some of the best to date for a durable VAD

  4. January 2016

    St. Jude Medical - CardioMEMS

    St. Jude Medical reveals that it is applying for a National Coverage Decision from CMS regarding its CardioMEMS product

    This is in response to unsupportive coverage decisions from Medicare Administrative Contractors in 2015

  5. February 2016

    SynCardia Systems, Inc. - SynCardia 50cc TAH

    Early findings of the SynCardia 50cc TAH IDE trial show the highest rate of implantations in females for a TAH

    FDA originally approved IDE study in March 2015

  6. March 2016

    Corvia Medical - IASD

    Receives IDE approval from FDA for a multicenter study of the IASD

    Company enters into an exclusive option-to-purchase agreement with an undisclosed strategic partner

  7. March 2016

    Teleflex - Arrow

    Teleflex announces a Class I worldwide recall of several IAB catheter kits

    The recall includes more than 47,000 units distributed worldwide

  8. March 2016

    Sunshine Heart - C-Pulse

    Sunshine Heart stops US COUNTER-HF study of ts C-Pulse therapy

    Announces plan for redesigned C-Pulse trial

  9. March 2016

    V-Wave

    FIM results are published for the V-Wave device

    V-Wave states that pivotal trials are being planned for North America, Europe, and Israel (V-Wave, press release, March 28, 2016)

  10. March 2016

    CardiacAssist/TandemLife

    CardiacAssist changes company name to TandemLife

    Begins offering portable temporary extracorporeal life support device that can offer both heart and lung support

  11. April 2016

    Abiomed - Impella

    The Impella 2.5, CP, 5.0, and LD catheters receive FDA approval for cardiogenic shock indication

    Labeling allows for use of Impella beyond intended 4- to 6-day period in cases of unforeseen circumstances
